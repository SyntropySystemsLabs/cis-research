# Milestone Papers & Research Artifacts

This section links major theoretical developments in the CIS framework to the research artifacts that document them.


## 2025 — Conceptual Formation
Exploratory Research Conversations

Early discussions explored the relationship between:
• informational structures
• constraint-based dynamics
• emergence of stable systems

These exploratory conversations served as the conceptual laboratory where the foundations of CIS were first developed.

Key themes explored:
• informational ontology
• emergence under constraint
• stability of semantic structures
• path dependence in interpretive systems


## December 2025 — First Explicit CIS Formulation

Research discussions at the end of 2025 produced the first explicit articulation of:

Constrained Informational Systems (CIS)

Major conceptual elements introduced during this stage:
• semantic field dynamics
• informational irreversibility
• constraint-driven state evolution
• path dependence in interpretive systems

This stage marks the birth of CIS as a named theoretical framework.


## January 2026 — Framework Expansion

The CIS framework expanded into a broader theoretical system including:
• semantic attractor dynamics
• informational collapse mechanisms
• constraint injection into informational fields

During this phase the theory began transitioning toward a general systems framework applicable to both natural and artificial informational systems.


## Mid-January 2026 — First CIS Preprint (Domain-Agnostic Foundations)
The first CIS research paper formalized the core framework and introduced the theory to the research community as a domain-agnostic systems model.

The paper focuses on:
• Constraint as a primitive of informational systems
• Meaning and structure emerging from constrained informational fields
• A general framework for analyzing stability and failure in informational systems

This work establishes the first formal publication of the CIS framework and defines its foundational theoretical principles.

Artifact

Nelson, D. S. (2026).
Constrained Informational Systems: A Formal Framework for Lawful Emergence Under Constraint
Zenodo DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18306843

https://zenodo.org/records/18306843


## March 2026 — CIS Preprint (First Domain Instantiation: AI Systems)
The second CIS paper applies the general framework to a specific technological domain: generative AI systems.

The paper focuses on:
• Constraint architectures in informational systems
• Implications for AI reasoning and reliability
• Structural failure modes in unconstrained generative systems

This work represents the first domain instantiation of the CIS framework, demonstrating its applicability to contemporary AI system design and governance.
