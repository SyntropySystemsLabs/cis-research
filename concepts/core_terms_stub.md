# Core Terms (Stub)

This document lists preliminary terms associated with the **Constrained Informational Systems (CIS)** research program.

The purpose of this file is to capture early definitions and conceptual placeholders so that terminology can stabilize as the research framework develops.

Definitions provided here are provisional and may evolve over time.

---

## Constrained Informational Systems (CIS)

A conceptual framework for analyzing systems in which informational processes are shaped by structural, epistemic, and operational constraints.

Within CIS, system behavior emerges not only from the information present in the system but from the **constraints governing how that information may be generated, interpreted, transmitted, and acted upon**.

---

## Constraint Architecture

The structural configuration of rules, limitations, and governing mechanisms that shape how information flows within a system.

Constraint architectures determine:

- what information can be produced
- what interpretations are permitted
- what decisions can be reached
- how system agents interact with informational states

---

## Epistemic Infrastructure

The collection of tools, processes, institutions, and frameworks used to generate, evaluate, and maintain knowledge within a system.

Examples may include:

- scientific institutions
- decision-making frameworks
- analytical tools
- reasoning protocols

---

## Reasoning Systems

Structured processes or mechanisms used by agents (human or artificial) to transform information into conclusions, decisions, or actions.

Reasoning systems may include:

- formal logical systems
- institutional decision processes
- algorithmic inference systems
- hybrid human–machine reasoning structures

---

## Interoperability

The ability of distinct systems, agents, or frameworks to exchange information in a manner that preserves meaning and supports coordinated reasoning or action.

Within the CIS research program, interoperability may include:

- communication between AI reasoning systems
- interaction between institutional decision frameworks
- translation between different logical or epistemic systems

---

## Informational Constraints

Limitations that shape how information may be:

- produced
- interpreted
- transmitted
- acted upon

Informational constraints may arise from:

- physical system limits
- institutional rules
- epistemic biases
- computational limitations
- governance structures

---

## Governance Layer

The set of mechanisms responsible for regulating system behavior and enforcing constraints within informational systems.

Governance layers may include:

- institutional oversight structures
- algorithmic policy frameworks
- regulatory environments
- internal system rules

---

## System Agents

Entities capable of interacting with informational systems by generating, interpreting, or acting upon information.

Examples may include:

- individual decision-makers
- organizations
- institutional actors
- automated or AI systems

---

## Notes

These definitions are intended as **initial conceptual anchors**.

They may evolve as the CIS research program progresses and as formal research papers establish more precise terminology.
